Density functional theory (DFT)¶

Modules: dft, pbc.dft

Overview¶

The DFT methods in modules pyscf.dft and pyscf.pbc.dft are implemented as derived classes of the base SCF class pyscf.scf.hf.SCF. The major modifications of DFT against HF include re-implementation of the get_veff() and energy_elec() methods. In addition to get_jk(), get_veff() also calls numint.nr_rks() (for spin-restricted cases) or numint.nr_uks() (for spin-unrestricted cases) to compute the exchange-correlation (XC) energy and potential. The XC energy is added to the total electronic energy in energy_elec().

Numerical integration¶

The XC functionals are evaluated on numerical quadrature grids. These grids are defined in modules pyscf.dft.gen_grid and pyscf.pbc.dft.gen_grid for molecules and solids, respectively. The actual methods to evaluate those XC functionals and their related integrals are implemented in modules pyscf.dft.numint and pyscf.pbc.dft.numint. For example, the XC energy and potential matrix for a given density matrix are computed by nr_rks() (or nr_uks()), which internally calls

  • eval_ao() – to compute the atomic orbitals (AOs) and their derivatives on the grid

  • eval_rho() – to compute the electron density and density derivatives on the grid

  • eval_xc() – to compute the XC energy and potential through an interface to the external library Libxc (pyscf.dft.libxc.eval_xc()) or XCFun (pyscf.dft.xcfun.eval_xc()).

Other convenient functions implemented in numint include

  • eval_mat() – evaluating the XC potential matrix with AO, electron density, and XC potential values on the grid as the input

  • nr_vxc() – evaluating the XC energy and potential matrix with the density matrix as the input

  • nr_sap_vxc() – evaluating the superposition of atomic potentials matrix, which is used as the initial guess for \(v_{\rm eff}\) when setting mf.init_guess to 'vsap'.

  • nr_rks_fxc(), nr_uks_fxc() – evaluating the XC kernel matrix
